PROJECT DESCRIPTION

Overview

Topic

The project is aimed to help users and provide them with support they need to address and seek help regarding any issues regarding their mental health and wellbeing and aims to be a stepping-stone or a first step to alleviating it. The project will consist of both a mobile and web application that would appeal to all age groups, focusing on app usability and efficiency to retrieve, store, track, and analyze user data. The app will be a professional online counselling platform that provides a low-cost alternative to face-to-face counselling through smartphones, personal computers, or tablets alike. Both the mobile and web apps will be a platform where groups and individuals can seek help and track their disparities in countries around the world. Like professional counselling websites like BetterHelp.com and Talkspace.com, this project aims to help individuals who may be suffering from mental health issues, as well as reducing the mental health disparities in countries around the world.

With our planned project focusing on helping users get help they need, in regards to a sense of personalization as a feature, its likely outcome is that it would attract and appeal more to users. As our project takes it a step further in user personalization and allows our services to be different and varied and unique with each user, it would allow our project to be recommended to people and as more and more users use our service means that what we have done behind the scenes in our project is working and have successfully served its main purpose to help our users with their mental health and make a difference.

Motivation

The motivation for this project stems from the pressing issue of mental health problems and issues that impacts people in this day and age where exposure to social media is prevalent. This project is important because mental illness has long been something that many people have been trying to combat, especially since the rise of technological advances and use such as smartphones and computers. In relation to the current coronavirus pandemic, more people are staying home more than ever, albeit with their family, friends or by themselves. With staying at home, people’s activities on social media and online platforms and increasing, meaning people have more exposure to screen time. According to the Journal of Medicine, the effects of public health emergencies on individuals and communities such as confusion, emotional isolation, and owing to economic loss “may translate into a range of emotional reactions”, such as distress and psychiatric conditions (Pfefferbaum and North 2-2-, p.510). Furthermore, it Is estimated that 20.1 percent of Australians had a “mental or behavioral condition” in 2017 to 2018 (Cook 2019, para. 6). In this current digital age and IT trends, applications that aim to provide users with help and services regarding mental health issues such as Code Blue that aims to provide teenagers who struggle with bullying, anxiety, and other similar applications that has a feature of using location services in order to suggest and provide support groups. If we were able to work on this project and produce a working application, it would show a future employer on how as developers, not only does it show how we made a complex application where every feature serves an important function but also the thought behind why we made the application where it makes an impact to an important issue and would help users who need it in a positive way.

Landscape

There are a limited number of products available within the online mental health sphere, some businesses within this niche include BetterHealth, GetMosh and Online Mental Health Online. In addition, there are existing mobile applications such as MY3, notOK, and Moodlift to name a few. These competitor applications that are mentioned have one feature that are common and it utilizes GPS and location services that allows users to send their current location to support groups or chosen friends or trusted contacts if they need someone to talk to. What sets it apart from other competitors or similar products and applications is that our platform differs from these listed as it allows for the user to input their wants and needs via questionnaire or survey which will then set the user up with a suitable mental health professional and also enables users to find what specific services they need. This is done to allow for the users to have greater access to help and comfortability while using our platform. Furthermore, this survey allows users to be paired with the ideal psychologist for them as seeking help from a psychologist and booking periodic schedules is a commitment and finding the right psychologist for a user where he or she is comfortable with and the users trust. This allows our application to go an extra mile to allow a sense of personalization for our users as they get the help that they need.

Aims

People may not be inclined to seek mental health treatment due to various reasons such as convenience, availability of counsellors in the area, financial reasons, or others. The main aim of this project is to increase more convenient options for groups and individuals who may need help in treating their mental health issues. This can be done by creating a low-cost alternative to face-to-face professional counselling through online platforms by the use of personal computers, tablets and smartphones, with a possibility of incorporating virtual reality. In addition, a secondary aim is to build a well-functioning and attractive mobile application for professional counselling where individuals can seek help and track their progress for their mental health.

Initial Application Elements and Plans for Changes

Navigation Bar

The original plan to include an efficient navigation bar was kept. In the app, users can access the home, progress, goals, meetings, and settings page from the bottom navigation bar. Elements such as FAQ, advice, language, about, account settings, and notifications can be modified and changed in the ‘Settings’ page.

Contents of the First page

One of the initial components that were planned as part of the app was having the first page of the app show the category the user identifies themselves in. For example, users can identify themselves as individuals, couples, teens, and children (under parental guidance), and corporate entities. This ensures that the app targets individuals and groups in society that are most prone to mental health. Once users have identified themselves in the category they fit in most, they will be led to a questionnaire that asks a series of demographic, psychographic, and geographic questions to more specifically identify the user’s needs. To add on, the survey would include questions regarding symptoms that may lead to anxiety, depression, and other mental problems. An optional test for users will be available for users to take such as the personality test or Big5 Test as additional information for counselors to gain insight on the user's personality and history to better help them.
Changes to make
The changes to be made to this original plan is making the signup and login page the content of the page when users first sign up, instead of the categories page and showing the signup page after users have entered the responses to the questionnaire. The questionnaire is a psychological test that will take place to identify the type of help the users are seeking and to redirect them to a health professional best fit for their issue. The users also have the option to choose the type of counselor they prefer and their expertise in fields such as depression, relationships, family conflict, trauma and abuse, and the like. Since the questionnaire is designed to identify the type of users and their needs, the categories page asking users to identify themselves as individuals, couples, teens, and children, and corporate entities is not needed.

Home Feature

The bottom navigation bar is planned to include the ‘Home’ function. On this page, several elements are to be included. Firstly, the ‘Book Consultation’ element, which shows a list of psychologists that the users can choose from. Users can book with a new psychologist or a psychologist they have previously met. Secondly, the ‘View Consultation History’ element, which shows details such as time, date, and duration of consultations with past psychologists. Thirdly, a ‘Find a mental health Professional’ element where users can choose from a list of mental health professionals that expertise in a range of mental health areas. The psychological testing questionnaire is accessed through this element. Fourthly, a ‘Written program and Regiment’ element. In this element, users can view their personalized program written by their chosen psychologist. This element aims to improve the user’s progress towards their desired goals. Lastly, the ‘Home’ page contains a timetable of consultations and agenda for current and future sessions.

Journaling Space Feature

In the initial description of the project idea, a journaling space feature was planned. It would provide a place for users to create journal entries where they can type, record, insert photos, and draw, which allows them to express their thoughts in different ways.
Changes to make
The journaling space feature is to be replaced with the ‘Goals’ page. Users can set goals according to what they want to work on after consulting with a therapist. On this page, users can set multiple goals, and create strategies and break them down into objectives to make it more achievable. The reason for this change is so the app is focused on the user’s development and outcome as setting goals creates proactiveness. Users can focus on what they need to work on to achieve the outcome that they desire.

Guided Meditation Exercise Page

Furthermore, in the initial description of the app, it includes a navigation bar element that contains Guided Meditation Exercises. The exercises are a form of meditation through breathing and visualization. Users can choose the type of exercise and the duration of 5, 10, 15, or 30 minutes. In addition, a reminder feature was planned to be included that sets daily, weekly, or monthly reminders of the app because it reminds users of exercises, tasks, meetings with therapists, and progress reports, etc. as people might be occupied with work, or simply forgetting to seek help.
Changes to make
The therapeutic exercise element has been removed and has to reduce the clutter within the app. Current applications such as Wysa and Moodpath are already emphasizing this component therefore it was decided that the project app should prioritize other elements. Instead, a ‘Progress’ page has been added to the replacement of the guided meditation page. The progress page includes both graphical and written progress reports of sessions held between users and their chosen mental health professionals. The report tracks how users feel in between sessions and how close they are to their goals.

Communication Method

The next feature that was kept from the initial project idea is a 1-on-1 session through online communication. The user can choose to communicate through a messaging room or a live chat session with their chosen mental health professional. The user can send video and audio recordings, upload files and documents, or call. The live chat session will be private and would require a passcode to enter for additional security. In the communication room, there will be two help features. The first help feature is an explanation of how the communication room works. The second help feature consists of a list of questions that the users may want to ask their psychologists in case they do not know how to get started with the type of questions to ask. The duration of the session can be stopped at any time and the user can request to change psychologists at any time via the ‘Home’ page.
Changes to make
The AI chatterbox that was planned in the initial description as a temporary replacement of unavailable health professionals is to be removed as it is an element that requires a high level of expertise to make the AI chatterbox accurately identify the emotional state of the user.

Meetings page

The meetings page is planned to be accessed through the bottom navigation bar. This page shows any upcoming meetings for the user with their chosen mental health professional. The name of the health professional will be shown, along with the date and time of the meeting shown below their names. When users press on any of the psychologist’s names, a description of their profile will be shown along with a button to book a session with them.

For the website, there has been no progress due to time constraints. There wasn’t enough time to design a wireframe and create a mockup for the website version of the app. Therefore, it was decided that prioritizing the mobile app was more important and moved the website project to a later date.
